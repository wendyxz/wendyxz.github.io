Art Portfolio




For a long time, I thought I was going to pursue post-secondary education and a career in the arts. My secondary education was about 50% visual arts and 50% academics. I had a portfolio ready to apply to a number of schools in the US. However, in grade 12 I started to really think about how I wanted to spend the next five years instead of what was "on track" for me. I decided that having a repeat of the last five years would be too uneventful, and I would be happier taking a break from something that I have been doing so long and venture into a completely unfamiliar area. I'm grateful for that decision - I've grew so much in the past year and half at UWaterloo, and I've done things I never thought I could have done.

Paint and Pastel

Here's to Edvard Munch, Monet, Impressionism, Van Gogh...and Sponge Bob's pineapple. These are done with oil paints and oil pastels, which is my favourite medium of all time. In hindsight, I should have spent less time with paints and more time with pastels.

Black and White

Though I have significantly more surviving colour pieces, I always had more of a talent when it came to black and white. The flower sketch was the fateful piece that got me into my high school. If I hadn't done it, I would have went to a regular high school, and perhaps ventured into programming earlier. I'm not sorry for the detour though, it was fun and the best time of my life. The tombstone charcoal piece is my favourite black and white drawing I've ever done. My art teacher in high school -- coolest teacher I ever had -- took us on a field trip to a cemetery, and this was born.
